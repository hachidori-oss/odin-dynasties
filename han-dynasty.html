The Han Dynasty

Period: 206 BCE – 220 CE

Imperial Legacy

The Han Dynasty was a pivotal era that solidified the foundations of Chinese culture. It was characterized by the adoption of Confucianism, the expansion of the empire's borders, and the opening of the Silk Road.

  • State Philosophy: Confucianism became the official state ideology.
  • Innovation: Major advancements in papermaking, the seismograph, and mathematics.
  • Identity: Its influence was so deep that the majority ethnic group in China still refers to themselves as "Han."

Modern Media & Interpretations

The drama and scale of the Han era have inspired countless modern works:

Television

The Emperor in Han Dynasty (Han Wu Da Di)

A highly-regarded historical drama detailing the life and reign of Emperor Wu, showcasing the peak of the dynasty's power.

Cinema

Red Cliff (Chi Bi)

Directed by John Woo, this epic focuses on the legendary Battle of Red Cliff during the end of the Han Dynasty.

Literature / Manhua

The Ravages of Time

A long-running manhua (comic) that offers a sophisticated retelling of the late Han era and the Three Kingdoms period.

Literature

Romance of the Three Kingdoms

One of the Four Great Classical Novels of Chinese literature, covering the fall of the Han imperial house.
