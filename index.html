The Major Dynasties

Han Dynasty

206 BCE – 220 CE

Often considered a golden age, the Han Dynasty established Confucianism as the state ideology and opened the Silk Road, connecting China to the West.

Tang Dynasty

618 – 907 CE

A period of high culture, flourishing arts, and religious tolerance. The Tang era saw the height of Chinese poetry and significant territorial expansion.

Song Dynasty

960 – 1279 CE

The Song Dynasty was a time of incredible technological innovation, including the invention of gunpowder, the compass, and movable type printing.

Ming Dynasty

1368 – 1644 CE

Known for its architectural achievements like the Forbidden City and the Great Wall, as well as its famous blue-and-white porcelain.

Qing Dynasty

1644 – 1912 CE

The final imperial dynasty of China. Founded by the Manchus, it expanded China's borders to their greatest extent before the fall of the imperial system.
